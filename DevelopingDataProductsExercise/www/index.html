Prediction Algorithm for Boston Housing Data

This data represents the Housing data for 506 census tracts of Boston from the 1970 census. The algorithm makes use of the following variables:

dis - weighted mean of distances to five Boston employment centres

cmedv - median value of owner-occupied homes in $1000s.


Usage

- Drag the slider to a specific distance value to display the predicted price of the house

- Model 1 represents the fitted linear regression of the housing value based on the distance

- Model 2 represents the fitted linear regression of the housing value based on the distance and a break at point 3


Source

Harrison, D. and Rubinfeld, D.L. (1978) Hedonic prices and the demand for clean air. J. Environ. Economics and Management 5, 81-102.

Belsley D.A., Kuh, E. and Welsch, R.E. (1980) Regression Diagnostics. Identifying Influential Data and Sources of Collinearity. New York: Wiley.
